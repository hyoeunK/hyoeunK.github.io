The Battleship Island

Film Marketing Senior Analyst, Summer 2017

Synopsis During the Japanese colonial era, roughly 400 Korean people, who were forced onto Battleship Island (‘Hashima Island’) to mine for coal, attempt to escape.

Responsibilities

  • Developed marketing and promotional strategies
  • Managed all aspects of the Artwork and Trailer

Marketing Overview During the process of marketing, the target audiences and the general public need to become more aware of the importance and meaning of this film as a historical movement. As part of that, on July 28, a special screen was held for UNESCO officials and diplomats in Paris to raise awareness of the hidden history. At the same time, as a blockbuster movie, we focused on production scale and action scenes. In the trailer, the scale of the movie was emphasized by realistic battle scenes and the impressive actor’s acting. As a result, the first trailer of this film hit 1 million views in 13 hours and it recorded the highest preference and recognition index evaluated by audiences. Also, this film marked the first time in the country that a movie had been released on more than 2,000 screens and overall admission was 6.58 million.
